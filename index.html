About

Ever since stepping foot in my first physics classroom during my junior year of high school, I have been hooked on solving problems. I would rush home to complete our physics assignments early so that I could explain the problems to a student who might post a question on our class’s online portal.

This passion only grew as I earned my B.S. in Physics at Cal Poly. I loved working on challenging problems with my peers in the physics student lounge. There is something so genuine and electric about working as a team, brainstorming ideas, and following trails of logic to uncover a solution. It is also at Cal Poly where I learned how to code. I admittedly struggled to find my footing at first but ultimately pursued a computational senior project in which I wrote code to analyze the physics governing a bead riding along a curved wire. My report has since been downloaded hundreds of times online by universities around the world.

My journey continued as I chose to enroll in a Physics PhD Program at University of Oregon. There, both the problems and my passion grew more intense. After two years of personal growth at grad school, I decided to leave my program and enter the workforce.

Once I discovered data science, I knew it was the career for me. It involves all of my passions: problem solving, coding, teamwork, and communicating your findings. I have since created several data science projects (posted on my GitHub) and graduated from Thinkful’s Data Science Mentor Program. Now, I am seeking a position as a full time data scientist. Discovering the hidden gems in a company’s data and turning that into actionable insight would be a dream job. I’m looking forward to the next leg of this journey.
